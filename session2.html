🎯 Learning Goals

By the end of this lesson, students will:

Review Week 1

Review what they learned in Week 1 (basic HTML structure).

Use Headings

Use headings (<h1>–<h6>) to organize text.

Create Lists

Create lists — both ordered (<ol>) and unordered (<ul>).

Text Formatting

Use text formatting tags (<strong>, <em>) to make words bold or italic.

Build Webpage

Build a webpage called "All About Me" that includes headings, paragraphs, and lists.

🧠 Key Vocabulary

Term Meaning
Heading A title or subtitle on a webpage (<h1>–<h6>)
Paragraph A block of normal text (<p>)
Ordered List A numbered list (<ol>)
Unordered List A bullet list (<ul>)
List Item Each point in a list (<li>)
Strong Makes text bold
Emphasis Makes text italic

💬 Warm-up (10 minutes)

Start with fun questions:

  • "Who remembers what <p> means?"
  • "How do we make a big title on a page?"
  • "What's inside the <body> tag?"

Open one student's Week 1 project and show it on screen.

Praise creativity — "This looks great! Let's make it even better today!"

🧩 Step-by-Step Lesson

🧱 Part 1: Headings & Paragraphs (10 minutes)

Explain:

"Websites use headings to make text easier to read — like titles and subtitles in a book."

Show example:

<h1>My Favorite Hobby</h1>
<h2>Playing Football</h2>
<p>I play football every weekend with my friends. I love being the goalkeeper!</p>

My Favorite Hobby

Playing Football

I play football every weekend with my friends. I love being the goalkeeper!

Discuss:

  • <h1> is the main heading (only one per page).
  • <h2>, <h3>... are smaller sub-headings.
  • <p> is for normal sentences.

Activity:

"Try to make a page with your favorite hobby. Use one <h1> and one <h2>."

🔢 Part 2: Lists (15 minutes)

Explain:

"Lists help us organize information — like your favorite foods or top games."

Show examples:

✅ Unordered (bullet) list

<h2>My Favorite Foods</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Pizza</li>
  <li>Burgers</li>
  <li>Ice Cream</li>
</ul>

🔢 Ordered (numbered) list

<h2>Top 3 Games</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Minecraft</li>
  <li>Roblox</li>
  <li>Fortnite</li>
</ol>

My Favorite Foods

  • Pizza
  • Burgers
  • Ice Cream

Top 3 Games

  1. Minecraft
  2. Roblox
  3. Fortnite

Ask:

  • "What's the difference between <ul> and <ol>?"
  • "What tag do we use for each list item?" → <li>

Activity:

"Make two lists — one ordered, one unordered. Choose your own topics!"

💪 Part 3: Text Formatting (20 minutes)

Explain:

"We can make text stand out using bold or italic tags."

Show:

<p>My favorite sport is <strong>football</strong> because it's exciting!</p>
<p>I also enjoy <em>drawing</em> and <em>reading books</em>.</p>

My favorite sport is football because it's exciting!

I also enjoy drawing and reading books.

💡 Tip:

  • <strong> means important text (bold).
  • <em> means emphasized text (italic).

Mini challenge:

"In your hobby paragraph, make one word bold and another italic!"

🏗️ Mini Project (15 minutes) — "All About Me" Page

Students create a web page about themselves.

Requirements:

  • ✅ One main heading (<h1>)
  • ✅ At least one subheading (<h2>)
  • ✅ Two paragraphs (<p>)
  • ✅ One unordered list (<ul>)
  • ✅ One ordered list (<ol>)
  • ✅ One <strong> and one <em> tag

Example:

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
  <head>
    <title>All About Me</title>
  </head>
  <body>
    <h1>All About Me</h1>
    <p>Hi! My name is <strong>Kareem</strong>. I love coding and learning new things!</p>

    <h2>My Hobbies</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Playing football</li>
      <li>Drawing</li>
      <li>Watching movies</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>My Top 3 Favorite Foods</h2>
    <ol>
      <li>Pizza</li>
      <li>Pasta</li>
      <li>Burgers</li>
    </ol>

    <p>I'm <em>super excited</em> to learn how to make cool websites!</p>
  </body>
</html>

🎉 Show & Tell (5 minutes)

Invite 2-3 kids to share their "All About Me" pages.

Ask fun questions:

  • "What's your favorite part of your page?"
  • "Who added a bold or italic word?"
  • "Who used an ordered list?"

👏 Celebrate effort and creativity — give stars or stickers!

🏡 Homework

  • Add one more list (e.g. "My Favorite Animals" or "Places I Want to Visit").
  • Add at least one new tag we learned today (heading, list, or text style).
  • Save the file as about.html in their project folder.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Mistake What Happens Fix
Forgetting to close <ul> or <ol> Everything after becomes a list Add </ul> or </ol>
Writing <li> outside of list Doesn't show properly Move <li> inside <ul> or <ol>
Using <strong> around heading Makes no visual difference Use <strong> inside paragraphs only
Forgetting <body> Page doesn't display Always wrap visible content inside <body>

🧰 Teacher Tips

  • Keep reviewing Week 1 tags — build familiarity.
  • Use examples from students' lives (foods, hobbies, cartoons).
  • Correct errors together on screen — show "what happens when we forget a closing tag."
  • Encourage teamwork — let them help each other debug.
  • Give them a short printed "HTML tags cheat sheet" with <h1>–<p>–<ul>–<li>.
